# When Stuck

A toolkit for breaking through when progress has stalled. Different kinds of stuckness require different approaches.

## See also

- [`sounding-board-mode`](../sounding-board-mode/SKILL.md) — talk it through
- [`simplification-cascades`](../simplification-cascades/SKILL.md) — maybe it's too complex
- [`inversion-exercise`](../inversion-exercise/SKILL.md) — flip the problem

## [GOAL]

Help the user diagnose why they're stuck and apply the right technique to get moving again.

## [PROCESS]

1. **Diagnose the stuckness**
   - Ask: "What are you stuck on, and how long have you been stuck?"
   - Listen for the type:
     - **Analysis paralysis**: Too many options, can't decide
     - **Blank page**: Don't know where to start
     - **Perfectionism**: Can't ship because it's not good enough
     - **Fear**: Know what to do but avoiding it
     - **Confusion**: Don't understand the problem well enough
     - **Energy**: Understand it but can't muster the motivation
     - **Dependency**: Waiting on something or someone

2. **Apply the matching technique**

   **For analysis paralysis:**
   - "If you had to decide in the next 5 minutes, what would you choose?"
   - "Which option is most reversible?"
   - "What's the cost of deciding wrong vs the cost of not deciding?"

   **For blank page:**
   - "Describe the finished version in one sentence"
   - "What's the smallest possible first step?"
   - "Start with the part you find easiest, not the beginning"

   **For perfectionism:**
   - "What's the minimum version that would be useful to someone?"
   - "What would 'good enough' look like?"
   - "Who is actually going to notice the gap between good enough and perfect?"

   **For fear:**
   - "What specifically are you afraid will happen?"
   - "What's the worst realistic outcome?"
   - "What would you tell a friend in this situation?"

   **For confusion:**
   - "What do you know for certain?"
   - "What would you need to learn to make this clear?"
   - "Who has solved a similar problem?"

   **For energy:**
   - "What would make this interesting or fun?"
   - "Can you delegate or trade this with someone?"
   - "What's the consequence of not doing this at all?"

   **For dependency:**
   - "What can you do while waiting?"
   - "Is there a way to remove the dependency?"
   - "Have you made the urgency clear to the person you're waiting on?"

3. **Get to a next action**
   - Whatever the technique reveals, end with one concrete next step
   - Make it small enough to start in the next 10 minutes
   - "What will you do right after this conversation?"

## [IMPORTANT]

- Most stuckness is a combination of types. Start with the dominant one.
- Ask 1-2 questions at a time. Don't dump the entire toolkit.
- Sometimes the answer is "stop and do something else" — that's legitimate.
- Don't be preachy. Be practical.
