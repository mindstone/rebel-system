# Example: External Strategic Partnership Prep

**Partnership Exploration - DataFlow Integration**
**Friday, January 19, 2025, 11:00 AM - 12:00 PM GMT (6:00 AM EST)**

---

## Executive Summary

Strategic partnership discussion with DataFlow (leading data integration platform, 50K+ customers, $400M ARR) about building a native two-way integration. Their VP Partnerships (Rachel Kim) reached out after seeing mutual customers request the integration, and both companies could benefit: we get access to their distribution channel (potential 5K qualified leads), they get differentiated analytics capability for their platform. Key considerations are technical complexity (estimated 3-4 month build), go-to-market alignment (co-selling vs. reseller model), and whether this displaces other integration priorities on our Q1 roadmap.

## Key Questions to Ask

**Strategic fit:**
- What's driving DataFlow's interest in this partnership right now?
- How many mutual customers are requesting this integration? What exactly are they asking for?
- What other analytics partners do you work with? (understand competitive dynamics)
- Where does this rank on your integration roadmap priority?

**Business model:**
- What partnership model are you envisioning? (co-selling, referral, reseller)
- What's your typical revenue share structure for technology partnerships?
- Would you want exclusivity in any category? (e.g., "exclusive analytics partner")
- How do you support partner GTM - co-marketing, sales enablement, lead sharing?

**Technical scope:**
- What does "native integration" mean from your perspective? (embedded UI, API sync, both?)
- What's your typical integration build timeline and technical support?
- Are there API rate limits or technical constraints we should know about?
- How do you handle authentication, data security, compliance for integrated partners?

**Validation:**
- Can we talk to 1-2 mutual customers about their integration needs?
- What's been your most successful technology partnership, and why did it work?
- What partnerships haven't worked well, and what went wrong?
