# Connected apps and trust

When a connected app (such as a business tool you have linked to Rebel) shows something interactive directly inside your conversation, Rebel makes sure you always know where it came from.

## Safe view: knowing what's yours and what's not

When an app renders an interactive view inside your conversation, the view is labelled **"From X · Safe view"** — where X is the name of the connected app. This is Rebel-owned chrome: the label appears on the view itself so you can see at a glance that it came from that specific app, not from Rebel.

An app cannot read this label or change it. The label is a Rebel guarantee, not something an app can influence or spoof.

## Connected app permissions

Permissions for connected apps are visible and manageable from **Settings → Safety → Connected app permissions**.

This panel shows every connected app that has access to your conversation context, what kind of access it has (which methods and tools it can call), and when you granted that access. From here you can review and revoke permissions whenever you want.

### How to revoke a connected app's permissions

1. Open **Settings → Safety → Connected app permissions**
2. Find the app you want to remove
3. Click **Remove access** next to that app

The app loses its access immediately. If you change your mind, you can re-authorise it from **Settings → Connectors** as normal.

Revoking an app's permissions does not break other connections or affect unrelated tools. Each app is independent.

## Keep it manageable

Only connect apps you actively use. If you connected something for a one-off task and haven't used it since, consider removing its access — it's one less thing to keep track of.

For a full list of all available connectors and how to connect them, see [MCPs, Tools & External Knowledge Sources](library://rebel-system/help-for-humans/mcp-connectors-tools-and-integrations.md).
