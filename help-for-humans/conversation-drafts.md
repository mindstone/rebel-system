# Conversation Drafts

Drafts let you capture thoughts without committing to a conversation. Start typing, change your mind, come back later—your words will be waiting.

## How Drafts Work

When you start typing a message in any conversation and navigate away before sending, Rebel saves your text as a draft. It's automatic—no save button required.

**The basics:**

- Drafts persist when you switch conversations
- Drafts persist when you close and reopen Rebel
- Each conversation can have one draft at a time
- Sending a message clears the draft


## Finding Conversations with Drafts

Two ways to spot conversations with unsent messages:

### Draft Indicator in Sidebar

Conversations with drafts show a small indicator in the history sidebar. Look for it when scanning your conversation list.

### Quick Navigation

Press **Ctrl+Tab** (or **⌘+Tab** on Mac) to cycle through conversations that have drafts waiting. This is the fastest way to hop between works-in-progress.


## Automatic Cleanup

Empty drafts don't stick around forever. If you delete all the text in a draft and navigate away, Rebel cleans it up automatically. Only drafts with actual content are preserved.


## Tips

- **Capture quick thoughts** — Start typing an idea in a conversation, then continue later when you have time to think it through
- **Work on multiple topics** — Keep drafts going in different conversations to context-switch without losing your place
- **Use Ctrl+Tab liberally** — It's the fastest way to see what you were working on across conversations
