Getting Started with Rebel

For: Anyone new to Rebel (no tech background needed!)
Reading time: 10-15 minutes
What you need: The Rebel desktop app installed

What is Rebel?

Rebel is your AI-powered assistant that lives on your computer. It knows about your work, your preferences, and your tools—and can handle tasks like prepping for meetings, drafting documents, or researching topics. Instead of starting from scratch every conversation, Rebel remembers context and learns over time.

Example: Say "Prep me for tomorrow's call with ACME Corp" and get a complete briefing with company research, talking points, and background on who you're meeting.

What Can Rebel Do?

Rebel gives you access to AI workflows (we call them "skills") that handle common tasks. Here are some examples:

Finding Information

You say: "Search Slack for discussions about the new browser extension"

Rebel: Searches through Slack channels and summarizes what it finds

Meeting Preparation

You say: "Prep me for my 2pm meeting with Sarah at TechCorp"

Rebel: Researches TechCorp, pulls up past conversations, creates a briefing document with talking points

Drafting Communication

You say: "Draft an email to Sarah about our Q4 renewal discussion"

Rebel: Creates a draft with relevant context and talking points

What Makes Rebel Different?

You might be thinking "I can already ask ChatGPT to do these things." Here's what's special about Rebel:

  • It remembers you: Your priorities, projects, preferences persist across conversations
  • It connects to your tools: Slack, Gmail, Calendar, Notion—Rebel can actually use them
  • It knows your context: Workspaces and spaces organize information by project or team
  • It speaks and listens: Full voice interaction, not just text
  • It asks before acting: Safety guardrails prevent accidental damage
  • It works in the background: Schedule recurring tasks like daily briefings

More details: See Why Rebel? for a full comparison with alternatives.

How Does It Work? (The Simple Version)

The Five-Second Explanation

Rebel is a desktop app that connects to a workspace folder on your computer. The workspace contains your files, skills (AI instructions), and memory. When you ask Rebel to do something, it reads the relevant context, runs the appropriate skills, and shows you the result.

Key Concepts (In Plain English)

What's a "Workspace"?

A workspace is a folder on your computer that Rebel uses as its home base. It contains:

  • rebel-system/: Core instructions and help docs (read-only)
  • Spaces: Folders for different areas of your work (personal, team, projects)
  • Skills: Reusable AI instructions for common tasks

If your team uses a shared workspace (via Google Drive, Dropbox, etc.), you'll all see the same shared content while keeping your personal stuff private.

What's a "Space"?

Spaces are areas within your workspace for different parts of your work. Think of them like folders with superpowers:

  • Chief-of-Staff: Your personal space (private to you)
  • Team spaces: Shared with your team
  • Project spaces: For specific initiatives

Each space can have its own skills, memory, and sharing settings.

What's a "Skill"?

A skill is like a recipe for the AI. It's step-by-step instructions for handling a specific task. For example, the "Meeting Prep" skill tells Rebel: "First, research the company. Second, check past interactions. Third, compile a briefing document."

Skills are just text files you can read and customize.

What's "Memory"?

Memory is what Rebel learns about you over time—your preferences, ongoing projects, key contacts. It's stored in your spaces and referenced automatically, so Rebel doesn't start from scratch every conversation.

You control what's in your memory, and Rebel asks before adding sensitive information.

What are "Connectors"?

Connectors let Rebel access your work tools—Gmail, Slack, Calendar, Notion, and more. Once connected, Rebel can actually DO things in these tools, not just talk about them.

Set these up in Settings → Connectors.

Getting Started: Your First 10 Minutes

Before You Start

Make sure you have:

  • Rebel desktop app installed
  • About 10 minutes of uninterrupted time
Step 1: Launch Rebel

Open the Rebel app. On first launch, you'll choose or create a workspace folder.

Step 2: Follow the Onboarding Checklist

Rebel walks you through setup with an interactive checklist:

  • Grant permissions (folder access, microphone if you want voice)
  • Create your profile
  • Quick tour of the interface
Step 3: Connect Your Tools (Optional)

Go to Settings → Connectors to connect services like Gmail, Slack, or Calendar. This lets Rebel access your real data.

See Connectors and Tools for details.

Step 4: Start Your First Conversation

Click New Conversation (or press Cmd/Ctrl+N) and try something like:

  • "What can you help me with?"
  • "Summarize my calendar for this week" (if calendar connected)
  • "Help me draft an email about [topic]"

Quick Question vs On the Case

Use the mode toggle in the composer:

  • Quick Question: Fast answers without tool access
  • On the Case: Full agent with tools and memory

Using Rebel Day-to-Day

The Basic Pattern

  1. Open Rebel
  2. Start a new conversation or continue an existing one
  3. Tell Rebel what you want in plain English
  4. Rebel does it and shows you the result

Voice Interaction

Rebel supports full voice interaction:

  • Listen mode: Press the global hotkey (default: Ctrl+Alt+Space) to speak your request
  • Speak mode: Rebel reads responses aloud

Configure voice settings and custom vocabulary in Settings → Agents & Voice.

Running Skills

To run a specific skill, mention it in your message:

Run the meeting prep skill for my call with Sarah at TechCorp

Or browse skills in the Library drawer.

Keeping Memory Updated

Rebel learns about you over time. You can:

  • Let Rebel update automatically: It asks before saving important context
  • Update manually: Say "Update my memory with [information]"
  • Review in Settings: See what Rebel knows in the Memory panel
