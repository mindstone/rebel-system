# Reply, Comment & Annotations

Select any text in Rebel — in conversations or documents — and turn it into action. Highlight a passage, ask Rebel about it, or leave a comment that becomes part of your next message.


## How It Works

When you select text in Rebel, a context menu appears with up to three options:

| Action | What it does |
|--------|-------------|
| **Copy** | Copies the selected text to your clipboard |
| **Ask Rebel** | Sends the selected text to the composer as a quoted reply, so you can ask a follow-up question about it |
| **Comment** | Adds an inline annotation — your note on that specific passage |

**In conversations**, right-click selected text to see these options.
**In documents** (Library file viewer), select text and the toolbar appears automatically above your selection.


## Commenting on Conversations

When Rebel replies with something you want to discuss further:

1. **Select the text** you want to comment on in Rebel's response
2. **Right-click** to open the context menu
3. Click **Comment** and type your note
4. Repeat on other parts of the response if you have multiple comments
5. A bar appears above the composer showing how many comments are ready
6. If you want, add extra context in the composer
7. Press the normal **Send** button — Rebel sends your pending comments along with anything you've typed

This is useful when you want to give feedback on multiple parts of a response at once — like reviewing a draft document or pointing out several things that need changing.

**Tips:**
- Comments are collected until you send them, so you can annotate multiple passages before responding
- There is no separate “Send comments” button — the usual **Send** action handles it
- Click **Clear** in the annotation bar to discard all pending comments
- Highlighted passages show where your comments are while you're building them up


## Commenting on Documents

When reading a file in the Library:

1. **Open a document** by clicking it in the Library panel
2. **Select text** you want to discuss — a floating toolbar appears
3. Choose **Ask Rebel** to quote it directly, or **Comment** to annotate it
4. After adding comments, click **Send to Rebel**
5. Choose where to send your comments:
   - **Continue file's conversation** — if you've discussed this file before, pick up where you left off
   - **Send to current conversation** — add your comments to whatever conversation is active
   - **Start new conversation** — fresh context for this discussion

Annotations persist in the document view and survive file moves, so your notes stay attached to the right passages.

Conversation comments also stick around beyond the immediate session. If you switch to a different conversation, restart the app, or if the app updates while you're using it, your comments stay exactly where you left them — you won't need to re-enter them.


## Ask Rebel (Reply)

The quickest way to discuss something specific. Select text and choose **Ask Rebel** — the selected passage appears as a quote in your composer, ready for you to type a question or instruction about it.

This works everywhere text is selectable: conversation replies, documents, and other content areas.
