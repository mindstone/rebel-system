# Scratchpad

The Scratchpad is Rebel's quick-capture modal for grabbing a thought before it escapes. It opens over whatever you're doing, so you can dump an idea, add a task, and move on with your life.

## Opening the Scratchpad

Press **⌘/Ctrl+Shift+N** from anywhere in Rebel.

That opens the Scratchpad as a modal window, so you can capture something quickly without leaving your current conversation or surface.


## Current layout

The Scratchpad is split into two columns:

- **Notes on the left** — freeform writing, quick capture, messy brain-dump material
- **Tasks on the right** — quick-add tasks and your upcoming task list

It auto-saves as you work, so you do not need to babysit a Save button like it's 2007.


## Notes panel (left)

Use the left side for rough notes, half-formed ideas, reminders, or text you want to sort out later.

- **Auto-save** — your scratchpad text is saved automatically
- **Private by default** — it lives in your Chief-of-Staff memory area
- **Recent files strip** — the footer shows recently updated memory notes so you can jump straight into them


## Turning scratchpad text into a proper note

When part of your scratchpad deserves promotion from "mess" to "real note":

1. **Select** the text you want to save
2. Click **New Note**
3. Rebel suggests a **folder** and **filename**
4. Review the suggestion and click **Create Note**

Rebel saves that selection as a proper memory note, then removes the selected text from the scratchpad so you do not end up keeping two copies around.


## Tasks panel (right)

The right side is for tasks that need a home.

- **Quick add** — type a task and press Enter or click **Add**
- **Optional due date** — add one if timing matters
- **Local tasks work out of the box** — you can use the panel without connecting anything
- **Todoist support** — if you connect Todoist, the panel shows your Todoist tasks instead

The goal is simple: capture the task now, organise your life later.


## Connecting Todoist

Todoist is now connected directly from inside the Scratchpad:

1. Open the Scratchpad
2. In the **Tasks** panel, open **Power up with Todoist**
3. Click **Connect**
4. Finish the sign-in flow

Once connected, the panel refreshes and starts showing your Todoist tasks there.


## Settings

Configure Scratchpad and tasks behavior:

1. Click the **gear icon** in the Scratchpad footer to jump straight to the relevant setting
2. Or open **Settings → Workspace → Scratchpad**
3. Add folders you want hidden from **Recent files** in the modal footer


## Tips

- Use the Scratchpad for **fleeting thoughts**, not polished writing
- Use **New Note** when something deserves to become a proper file
- Keep the task list lightweight — if a task needs a full plan, open a conversation and let Rebel help
- If your recent files feel noisy, trim them via **Settings → Workspace → Scratchpad**
