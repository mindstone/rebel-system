# Getting Started with Rebel

Welcome to Rebel. Let's get you set up and running your first conversation.

---

## 1. Open Rebel and Pick Your Workspace

When you first launch Rebel, you'll choose (or create) a **workspace**—a folder where Rebel keeps your spaces, skills, and memory. This is your home base.

- Pick an existing folder, or let Rebel create one for you
- If your team uses a shared workspace (like a synced Google Drive or Dropbox folder), you can connect that too—see [Shared Folders](library://rebel-system/help-for-humans/space-shared-folders.md)

Rebel stores its own app data separately from your workspace. See [Where Rebel Stores Things](library://rebel-system/help-for-humans/where-rebel-stores-things.md) for details.

---

## 2. Walk Through the Setup Wizard

Rebel guides you through setup with a short step-by-step wizard. You'll see Rebel's homepage in the background while you work through the main steps:

1. **Spaces** — Set up your workspace and connect cloud storage (Google Drive, OneDrive, or Dropbox) so Rebel can access your files
2. **Connect your AI** — Choose the provider card that matches how you want to run Rebel: **ChatGPT Pro**, **OpenRouter**, or **Anthropic**
3. **Talk to Rebel** — Send a first message so Rebel can confirm everything is working
4. **Connectors** — Link your email, calendar, chat, and other services so Rebel can work with them. Browse by category or search for specific tools

When you finish, Rebel prepares your workspace and reveals the homepage with a guided tour of the key features.

The wizard appears automatically on first launch. If you want to revisit it later, go to Settings → Relaunch Onboarding.

---

## 3. Add More Connectors Later

You connected your core tools during setup, but you can add more anytime in **Settings → Connectors**.

- Browse available connectors and click to enable
- Some connectors require authentication (you'll be guided through it)
- You can add or remove connectors anytime

For more details, see [Connectors and Tools](library://rebel-system/help-for-humans/mcp-connectors-tools-and-integrations.md).

---

## 4. Start Your First Conversation

Now you're ready to talk to Rebel:

1. Click **New Conversation** (or press **⌘/Ctrl+N**)
2. Type or speak your request
3. Rebel will respond—and may ask permission before taking actions

Try something like:
- "What can you help me with?"
- "Summarize my calendar for this week" (if calendar is connected)
- "Help me draft an email about [topic]"

**Tip:** For sensitive work, flip **Privacy Mode** on from the conversation header — Rebel will ask before every tool use and memory write. See [Session Modes](library://rebel-system/help-for-humans/session-modes.md) for the other modes.

---

## 5. Add More Spaces (Optional)

As you use Rebel, you can organize your knowledge into **spaces**—areas for different projects, teams, or contexts. Each space can have its own skills and memory.

Your personal **Chief-of-Staff** space is created automatically. You might add:
- A shared team space
- Project-specific spaces
- Personal spaces for family or side projects

See [Understanding Spaces](library://rebel-system/help-for-humans/spaces.md) for more.

---

## Features You'll Notice

A few things to look for as you explore:

- **Drafts** — Start typing a message, switch conversations, come back later—your draft is still there. Use **Ctrl+Tab** to cycle through conversations with drafts.
- **Starred** — Star important conversations to keep them at the top of your history sidebar.
- **Actions** — Save tasks for later. When something comes up but you're not ready to act, add it to your Actions.
- **Homepage** — Your landing surface for today’s priorities, recent chats, and coach recommendations.

---

## Alternative: Using Cursor or Claude Code

If you prefer working in an IDE, Rebel can expose its capabilities via MCP for external tools. See Settings → Connectors → External MCP Access for setup. Note: most Rebel features work best in the desktop app.
