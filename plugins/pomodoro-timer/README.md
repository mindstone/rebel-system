# Focus Timer

A timer that runs while you do the thing. It counts down. You do work. Repeat.

## What it does

- Counts down from a focus duration you pick (5, 15, 25, 45, or 60 minutes)
- Shows a circular progress ring so you can see the time disappearing
- Tracks how many sessions you've completed
- Pauses and resumes; remembers your session count between launches

## When to use it

When the work is clear and the willpower isn't. When you need a visible nudge that says "this slice of time is for one thing." When your calendar gave up on you and you need a personal one.

## Data and control

This plugin only stores how many sessions you've completed and what duration you last picked — nothing else. Numbers stay on your machine. You can turn it off, edit its source, fork it, or delete your copy from the Library lens whenever you like.

## For tinkerers

This started as a Rebel built-in plugin and lives in `Chief-of-Staff/plugins/pomodoro-timer/`. The whole thing is one `.tsx` file — short, readable, and a fine starting point if you want to build a tiny tracker or stopwatch of your own. Edit it, fork it, or delete it. Yours to use.
